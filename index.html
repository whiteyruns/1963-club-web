The Story

1963. Before Vegas Went Corporate.

There was a time when deals were made at small tables. When the bar mattered more than the DJ. When the room was thick with smoke and nobody took photos.

1963 Club is a return to that era.

Inspired by the golden age of Las Vegas and the intimacy of the world's great private clubs, it exists for those who prefer velvet over volume and presence over performance.

The Dinner

It Begins Upstairs.

Before the room below, there is the table above. A steakhouse in the El Cortez tradition — prime cuts, candlelight, unhurried service. The kind of dinner where the check is never mentioned and the wine keeps arriving.

The steakhouse is led by Chef David Robbins — Hawaiian-born, globally trained, with a palate shaped by years across Peru, Polynesia, and the great steakhouse traditions of the American West. His menu is built around prime beef, whole fish, and tableside presentations designed for the room, not the camera.

This is the threshold. The evening's first act. Members dine with their guests, and the conversation begins to turn — toward the cards, toward the night ahead.

The dinner is the invitation. The descent is the reward.

The Room

Designed for Spending Time — and Money.

4,000 square feet of layered intimacy.

The Bar

An oval bar wrapped in leather and brass. Backlit shelves of rare Scotch, bourbon, and vintage wine. Tableside martini service.

The Baccarat Salon

A statement table at the center of the room. A private high-limit salon just beyond. Reservations accepted.

The Pool Room

One tournament-grade table. Deep leather seating. Cigars permitted. The kind of game that lasts all night.

Passed Apps

Chef Robbins' steakhouse kitchen sends down a rotating selection of late-night bites — wagyu sliders, chilled shellfish, steak tartare on toast points, truffle fries. Nothing plated, everything passed. The table never interrupts the game.

The Humidor

Climate-controlled. Curated global selection. Member lockers available. A place to sit, smoke, and say nothing for a while.

No dance floor. Lively, but always seated.

The Pour

The Cellar & The Cabinet

The program is classic, not clever.

Scotch & Whisky

Aged expressions and collector-level allocations. Macallan 25, Dalmore Cigar Malt, closed-distillery bottlings. Poured two fingers at a time, never rushed.

Bourbon

Rare releases and private barrel selections. Pappy Van Winkle, George T. Stagg, allocated pours that don't make it to most back bars. Ask the bartender what just arrived.

Wine

First-growth Bordeaux. Elite Napa Cabernets. Burgundy by the bottle. Large-format magnums for the table. The cellar is small, curated, and turning constantly.

Cocktails

Martini. Manhattan. Negroni. Vesper. Old Fashioned. Five drinks, executed precisely. Tableside martini service available. No foam, no smoke, no theatre — just the drink.

The bartender remembers your drink. The second one arrives without asking.

The Table

Gaming, Without the Floor.

1963 Club is not a public casino. It is a private gaming salon designed for discretion and elevated play.

Baccarat anchors the room.

Private reservations available. No crowds. No chaos. Just felt, chips, and silence when it matters.

The Code

Membership Is Not Purchased. It Is Considered.

1963 Club operates under a simple code:

  • Discretion.
  • Respect.
  • No photography.
  • Dress expectations enforced.
  • Referral required.
  • Members may host guests.

The room exists for those who understand it.
